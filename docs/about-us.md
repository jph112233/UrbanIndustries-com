## Company Overview

Urban Industries is a diversified manufacturing corporation headquartered in Galion, Ohio, operating three facilities with over 175,000 square feet of combined manufacturing space.

---

## History

### Founding Era (1940s-1950s)
Joe Urban, a Swiss immigrant and commercial photographer, developed a self-supporting aluminum awning and established the company in Galion before WWII. He expanded the factory seven times throughout the 1950s, growing the workforce to over 60 employees by 1957 and opening a California plant in Azuza.

### Ownership Transitions (1963-1981)
Following Joe Urban's death in 1963, Jacque Jones acquired the company. Ed Rieke and partners purchased the struggling business in 1976 (then down to 6 employees) and successfully revitalized operations through focused management and reinvestment.

### Modern Expansion (1992-Present)
The company diversified significantly:
- 1992: Launched the Bulk Bag division
- 1998: Entered appliance and transportation parts fabrication
- Achieved national accreditation on major product lines
- 2017: Ned Rieke (Ed's son) assumed the CEO role, continuing the company's adaptive strategy and customer-focused approach
